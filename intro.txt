Based on the uploaded project presentations and proposal documents, the **Machine Learning Group (MLG)** at ULB is executing the **EDITS** project (*Enhancing DIgital TwinS with scalable and interpretable AI*), which is funded under the **WEL-T Advanced Investigator Programme** [extracted.md]. 

The primary mission of MLG within this project is to bridge the gap between theoretical machine learning and the practical deployment of Digital Twins (DTs) by integrating physics-based modeling with adaptive, scalable, and causal AI [extracted.md].

The work at MLG is organized into specific methodological pillars and practical case studies:

---

### I. Methodological & Technical Pillars

1.  **Data Infrastructure & Streaming (Led by Carlo Fabrizio & Gianmarco Paldino)**
    *   **Intelligent Databases:** Researching adaptive time-series databases (like TimescaleDB and InfluxDB) that utilize **Lifelong Learning (LL)** to prevent "catastrophic forgetting" and gracefully handle dataset shifts in high-fidelity streaming environments [extracted.md].
    *   **Data Flow Orchestration:** Building a scalable, loosely coupled client-server architecture (using a Lambda architecture and message brokers like Apache Kafka) to ingest, pre-process, and store both real-world and synthetic data streams [extracted.md].

2.  **Calibration, Data Assimilation & Learning (Led by Carlo Fabrizio & Pascal Tribel)**
    *   Calibrating complex multi-physics models with high-dimensional streams of real data [extracted.md].
    *   Integrating physical models with data-driven ML surrogate models (proxies of physical systems) to enable real-time tracking, even under non-stationary conditions and wear-and-tear [extracted.md].

3.  **Foundational Models & Transfer Learning (Led by Cédric Simar)**
    *   Developing "Foundational Models" for Digital Twins to avoid building virtual models from scratch [extracted.md].
    *   Applying transfer learning so that a DT calibrated for one physical asset (e.g., a specific wind turbine or a city neighborhood) can transfer its learned dynamics to a new, similar asset [extracted.md].

4.  **Causal Discovery & Counterfactual Reasoning (Led by Gianmarco Paldino)**
    *   Moving beyond purely predictive AI to incorporate causal reasoning [extracted.md].
    *   Enabling **"What-If" and counterfactual analysis**—giving the Digital Twin the ability to replay past configurations (like a traffic jam or a wind turbine fault) to evaluate how alternative human interventions or automated controls would have changed the outcome [extracted.md].

### II. Applied Case Studies

To validate and assess this AI-driven methodology, MLG is deploying and testing Digital Twins across several real-world domains [extracted.md]:

*   **1. Energy Twin / Virtual Power Plant (VPP) (Gianmarco Paldino)**
    *   Developing DT architectures to manage distributed energy resources (solar, wind, load, batteries) [extracted.md].
    *   Utilizing multivariate forecasting to predict community-level energy dynamics and running linear programming to optimize battery scheduling, reducing energy costs and balancing the grid [extracted.md].
    *   Simulating wind farm operations under extreme weather to predict maintenance needs and assess risk [extracted.md].

*   **2. Traffic Twin / Mobility (Eladio Montero Porras)**
    *   Evaluating the impact of urban roadworks, events, and detours on vehicular traffic in Brussels and Namur [extracted.md].
    *   Combining real-world sensor data with micro-simulations (using the SUMO simulator) to assist public authorities and mobility managers in selecting optimal road deviation plans [extracted.md].

*   **3. Robotics & Rehabilitation / Human Twin (Cédric Simar)**
    *   *Collaborative Robotics:* Creating a digital twin for workstations where a physical robotic arm assists workers. The twin collects biosignal (EMG) fatigue data and runs what-if scenarios to determine real-time support strategies [extracted.md].
    *   *Rehabilitation:* Building subject-specific musculoskeletal models using motion tracking and EMG data to help therapists identify rehabilitation exercises that minimize injury risk [extracted.md].

*   **4. Toy Case Studies**
    *   Utilizing simplified systems—such as the digital twin of a laboratory plant—to define and test core DT methodologies through master's thesis research [extracted.md].
